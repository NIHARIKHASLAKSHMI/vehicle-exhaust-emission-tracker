Vehicle Exhaust

Vehicle exhaust emissions consist of the remnants of atmospheric air, carbon dioxide (CO2), water vapor, and numerous compounds in either particulate or gaseous form, the majority of which result from the incomplete combustion of fuel.

Vehicle Exhaust

A leak in exhaust system can allow carbon monoxide, a colorless, odorless, and highly toxic gas, to enter the passenger cabin. Breathing in carbon monoxide can lead to symptoms like dizziness, nausea, headaches, and, in severe cases, even death.

Exhaust Gas

Exhaust gas or flue gas is emitted as a result of the combustion of fuels such as natural gas, gasoline (petrol), diesel fuel, fuel oil, biodiesel blends, or coal.

Motor Vehicle

Motor vehicle exhausts also produce greenhouse gases that contribute to climate change. The main greenhouse gas produced by vehicles is carbon dioxide (CO2), but they also produce nitrous oxide and methane.

Gasline Engine

Gasoline engine exhaust is a mixture of many chemicals. It is produced when engines, including those in cars, trucks, boats, and motorized equipment, burn gasoline.
